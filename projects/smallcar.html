Small RC car

Description

The aim of this project was to design and make a prototype of a remote control car that would be as small as possible, mainly focusing on the chassis design.
I achieved the small form factor by 3D printing a chassis where all the parts, including the four motors and the batteries, slot into.
It can be controlled with an Xbox remote and has a live camrea feed.

Skills

  • Practical 3D printing for use in a larger sytem
  • Compact design
  • Remote control of systems using WiFi
  • Design analysis

Closer look

I chose a simple design for the car so it would be small and need less development work.
A custom PCB could have been made to reduce size further, however this project was aimed at using common, readily available parts.

Two Raspberry Pis are used for this project. One has a controller connected to it, the other sending pulse width modulation (PWM) signals to the motor driver.
They communicate over WiFi and can send a live video feed from the car.

Differential steering is used, which is when the two sides of the car spins at different rates, similar to how tanks steer.
This removes the need for extra items to be added to turn the wheels which would take up space.

I tested the different parts individually to make sure that they were robust and fitted properly.
The design allowed for the parts to slot in and not need any other fixings.
The amount of support material was minimalised, only one part needed support material. This lowered printing time and reduced waste.
